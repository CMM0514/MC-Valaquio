Introduction: Chapter 1

Foundation and Framework of the Internship Program

This section presents the importance, objectives, and official time and place of the On-the-Job Training experience.

A

Importance of Internship

Real workplace learning

The importance of an internship, or On-the-Job Training, is that it gives students real-world experience in their chosen field. It allows them to apply the knowledge and skills they learned in school into actual work situations, making learning more practical and meaningful.

Internship also helps students develop important skills such as communication, teamwork, discipline, time management, and professionalism. It exposes them to the real working environment, helping them understand workplace expectations and responsibilities.

Most importantly, internship prepares students for their future careers by building their confidence and experience, making it easier for them to adjust when they enter the workforce after graduation.

B

Objectives of Internship

Training goals

  1. To provide students with real-world experience related to their chosen field and help them apply the knowledge they learned in school into actual work situations.
  2. To develop and enhance students' technical, practical, and professional skills through hands-on training and exposure to workplace tasks.
  3. To improve important work values such as discipline, responsibility, communication skills, teamwork, and time management.
  4. To help students become familiar with the workplace environment, understand organizational procedures, and learn how to handle different tasks and challenges.
  5. To prepare students for their future careers by building their confidence, experience, and readiness for employment after graduation.

II. Company Profile

Occidental Mindoro Electric Cooperative, Inc.

OMECO is a distribution utility serving mainland Occidental Mindoro through socially responsible, reliable, and community-centered electric service.

A

Nature of the Agency

Origins, service growth, and recovery

Origins & Mandate
  • 1969: Presidential Decree No. 269 created the National Electrification Administration to organize electric cooperatives and pursue municipal electrification.
  • 1974: OMECO was organized through Atty. Eufrocino L. Ramos of PECT.
Early Operations
  • March 1974: A PHP 6.9 million initial loan was signed with NEA, with an interim Board led by Dr. Rodrigo M. David.
  • May 7, 1974: OMECO took over San Jose Electric Company for PHP 620,000 and began with nine employees.
  • 1975: General Manager Engr. Alberto A. Guiang acquired two 350kw generator sets, built lines, and held the first Electrification Week.
Expansion & Projects
  • 1978: The main office moved to Pulang Lupa, Central.
  • 1980: Mamburao was energized and Silahis Electric Company was bought for PHP 760,000.
  • 1981: Engr. Romeo V. Razo became General Manager to address the cooperative's negative loan balance.
  • The Dendro Thermal Plant in Paclolo, Magsaysay became the first of its kind in Asia, though it later failed after the plantation source was destroyed.
Financial Crisis & Recovery
  • 1983-1984: OMECO nearly collapsed, received no NEA aid, and wages went unpaid.
  • December 25, 1983: Col. Zoilo M. Perez became General Manager, paid staff from personal funds, and refused salary for six months.
  • March 3, 1984: Operations resumed and the cooperative recovered through frugality, earning Perez the nickname "Wonder Lolo."
Later Developments
  • 1995: Alex C. Labrador became the first rank-and-file employee to rise to General Manager.
  • 1996: Botasan, Monte Claro, and Sitio Cebu were energized, while the IPC plant in Bubog moved to 24/7 operations.
  • 1997: Members voted 444-104 to stay with NEA over CDA to keep tax exemptions and continue serving remote barangays.
  • 1999-2000: OMECO celebrated its 25th anniversary, held a Sports Fest, and continued focusing on quality service for remote barrios.

B. Mission

Service with responsibility

A distribution utility delivering service in a socially responsible manner for the improvement of the quality of life of the people of mainland Occidental Mindoro.

C. Vision

Reliable and sustainable power

To be a financially stable electric cooperative providing quality, reliable, and sustainable electric service using world-class, state-of-the-art, and cost-effective systems and facilities operated by highly competent and motivated officers and employees.

D

History / Background of the Agency

Development of OMECO

OMECO, or Occidental Mindoro Electric Cooperative, Inc., was organized in 1974 through the initiative of Atty. Eufrocino L. Ramos under Presidential Decree No. 269, which created the National Electrification Administration in 1969 to oversee rural electrification nationwide. With an initial PHP 6.9 million loan from NEA in March 1974, OMECO's interim board headed by Dr. Rodrigo M. David purchased the San Jose Electric Company for PHP 620,000 and formally took over operations on May 7, 1974.

Under its first General Manager, Engr. Alberto A. Guiang, the cooperative expanded from just nine employees, acquired two 350kw generators, built transmission and distribution lines, and launched aggressive membership drives. Public awareness efforts included the first Electrification Week in 1975 and annual membership meetings that encouraged community involvement through the bayanihan system.

Through the late 1970s and 1980s, OMECO continued expanding its coverage, energizing Mamburao in 1980 and acquiring Silahis Electric Company that same year. The cooperative built its main office in Pulang Lupa, Central in 1978 and experimented with the Dendro Thermal Plant in Paclolo, Magsaysay, the first of its kind in Asia, though the Ipil-Ipil plantation for fuel was later destroyed.

By December 1983, OMECO faced a severe financial crisis with no aid from NEA and unpaid wages. Col. Zoilo M. Perez (Ret.) took over as General Manager, paying employees from his own funds and forgoing his salary for six months. Through strict frugality and employee cooperation, operations resumed in March 1984 and the cooperative eventually recovered.

In 1995, Alex C. Labrador became the first rank-and-file employee to rise to General Manager, continuing energization efforts in remote areas like Botasan, Monte Claro, and Sitio Cebu in 1996. In a membership vote on February 21, 1997, 444 voted to stay with NEA versus 104 for CDA, preserving OMECO's tax exemptions and commitment to serve far-flung barangays.

OMECO celebrated its 25th anniversary in 1999 and by 2000 remained focused on its mission of providing dependable service to member-consumers and lighting up remote communities, guided by its established vision, mission, and goals despite ongoing challenges.
